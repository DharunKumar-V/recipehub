Classic Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste (or finely minced ginger and garlic)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste

For the Butter Chicken Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste (or finely minced ginger and garlic)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes or tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Optional for added richness:
  • 2 tablespoons cashew paste or almond paste (blend soaked cashews or almonds with a bit of water to make a paste)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, chili powder, and salt.
    2. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    1. Preheat your grill, oven, or stovetop skillet. Cook the marinated chicken until fully cooked, with a slight char or browning. If grilling or broiling, cook for about 5-7 minutes per side. If using a stovetop skillet, cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until done.
  3. Make the Sauce:
    1. In a large pan or skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
    2. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until soft and golden brown.
    3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
    4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes or tomato puree and cook for 5-7 minutes until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the tomato.
    5. Add the cumin, coriander, garam masala, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
    6. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream and cashew or almond paste if using. Simmer for a few minutes to blend the flavors.
    7. Add the cooked chicken pieces to the sauce and simmer for another 5-10 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
  4. Serve:

    Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and serve with naan bread or rice.
